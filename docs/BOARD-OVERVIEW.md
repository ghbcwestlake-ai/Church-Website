# Good Hope Missionary Baptist Church — Website Overview for the Board

> **Purpose of this document:** a plain-language overview of the new church
> website, written for board members (non-technical). It is structured slide by
> slide so it can be turned directly into a slide presentation.
>
> **Brand colors:** navy blue (#1F4173) and gold (#C9A24A), with cream/off-white
> and a touch of red. Tone: warm, plain-spoken, dignified — "come as you are."
>
> **Design note for the slide tool:** keep it clean and simple. Generous white
> space, large readable text (the audience is older), one idea per slide. Use the
> navy/gold/cream palette. Speaker notes are included where helpful.

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title

# Good Hope Missionary Baptist Church
## Our New Website — An Overview for the Board

*A warm front door for everyone God sends our way.*

---

## SLIDE 2 — What It Is

### Think of it as our front door — online

- Today, when someone hears about a church, the **first thing they do is look it up** — usually on their phone, often before they ever call or visit.
- Our website is that **first impression**. It's where a stranger decides, *"Is this a place for me and my family?"*
- It is one simple, scrolling page — easy to read top to bottom, no confusing menus to get lost in.

> Speaker note: This is the modern version of someone driving by to see the building. Now they "drive by" on their phone first.

---

## SLIDE 3 — Why It Matters

### People decide fast — and online

- Most people visiting a church for the first time **looked at the website first.**
- They form an opinion in just a **few seconds.**
- If they can't quickly find *when we meet, where we are,* and *what to expect,* they move on — and we never knew they were interested.
- A clear, warm website means **more first-time visitors walking through our doors on Sunday.**

---

## SLIDE 4 — The One Goal

### Everything serves one purpose

> **Turn a stranger into a visitor, and a visitor into part of the Good Hope family.**

- Every part of the site is there to gently move someone one step closer to **showing up on Sunday.**
- If something on the page doesn't help with that, we take it off. Simple and clean wins.

---

## SLIDE 5 — How We Designed It (the thinking)

### We give people what they're looking for — right away

A first-time visitor is quietly asking four questions:

1. **Who are you?** (Is this church for me?)
2. **When and where do you meet?**
3. **What happens if I show up?** (What do I wear? Where do I park? Will I feel welcome?)
4. **How do I take a next step without pressure?**

The website answers all four **in the first few seconds** — no hunting required.

---

## SLIDE 6 — Built for Phones First

### Most people will see it on their phone

- The majority of visitors will open it on a **cell phone**, not a computer.
- So we built it to look clean and be easy to tap on a phone first — then it works just as well on tablets and computers.

---

## SLIDE 7 — A Clear Next Step for Everyone

### Four welcoming paths, right at the top

The moment someone arrives, they can choose what fits them:

- **Plan Your Visit** — for someone ready to come
- **New Here?** — for someone just getting to know us
- **Need Prayer?** — for someone who needs prayer today
- **Talk With Us** — for someone who simply wants to reach a real person

*No one feels lost, and no one feels pressured.*

---

## SLIDE 8 — The "Connect With Us" Section

### Meeting people where they are

We rebuilt this part to welcome **newcomers**, not just current members:

- **Request prayer** — quickly and privately, right on the page (no phone call needed)
- **Watch a service online** — get a feel for Good Hope from home before visiting
- **Find your place** — see the ministries and where they might belong
- **Weekly encouragement** — sign up to receive a short word of encouragement and Scripture each week — *a gentle first step, even before they visit*

> Speaker note: We intentionally removed "Give" from this first impression — we never want a newcomer's first experience to be an ask for money.

---

## SLIDE 9 — Keeping It Honest and Timeless

### We removed the 150th-anniversary focus

- The site originally centered on our **150th celebration.**
- Since the website won't be finished and shared before the celebration, leading with it would quickly feel **out of date.**
- We moved our rich **150-year history** to the "**Our Story**" page, where it belongs — honored, but not the headline.
- The main site is now **evergreen**: it will still feel current next year and the year after.

---

## SLIDE 10 — The Feel

### Warm, plain-spoken, and real

- The tone is **"come as you are"** — a real church family, not a slick corporate brand.
- Our colors and logo give it a look that is **simple, clean, and dignified.**
- Every word is meant to make a stranger feel **invited.**

---

- Our history preserved on its own page

## SLIDE 13 — The Bigger Picture

### The website opens the door. People walk them through it.

- The website's job: help a stranger feel welcome and **take a first step.**
- But a button click isn't a relationship. **What turns interest into attendance — and attendance into belonging — is a real person following up.**
- So we need two things working together: **the website** *and* **a simple plan for who responds.**

---

## SLIDE 14 — From Stranger to Family

### Every visitor is somewhere on one journey

1. **Stranger** — has never heard of us
2. **Curious** — found the website, looking around
3. **Takes a step** — requests prayer, plans a visit, or reaches out
4. **First visit** — shows up on a Sunday
5. **Returns** — comes back again
6. **Family** — belongs here, connected and growing

*Our goal is to gently help people move from one stage to the next — never rushed, never pressured.*

---

## SLIDE 15 — What Happens When Someone Takes a Step

### Every path leads to a real, caring response

| When a visitor… | The website… | Then a person… |
|---|---|---|
| **Plans a visit** | Captures their info privately | Reaches out personally, answers questions, watches for them Sunday |
| **Requests prayer** | Sends the request straight to us | Prays for them — and follows up with care |
| **Wants to talk** | Passes along their message | Calls or emails them back within a couple of days |
| **Watches online** | Lets them experience a service from home | Invites them to come in person |
| **Signs up for weekly encouragement** | Adds them to the list | Sends a short, warm word each week — keeping them close until they're ready |

*The promise: no message ever goes unanswered.*

---

## SLIDE 16 — Who Carries the Follow-Up

### This only works if someone owns it

We need a **point person for outreach** (or a small welcome team) responsible for:

- Checking for new requests **regularly**
- Responding **within 48 hours** — warmly and personally
- Passing **prayer requests** to Pastor Tim / the prayer team
- **Welcoming first-time visitors** and following up after their visit
- Sending the **weekly encouragement** note

**Proposed:** [Your name] leads this to start, with help as it grows.

> Speaker note: This is the single most important decision for making the website actually produce visitors. A beautiful site with no one answering is a doorbell no one hears.

## Appendix — Quick Facts (for Q&A, not a slide)

- **Format:** one scrolling page, plus an "Our Story" history page.
- **Where it lives:** hosted online; updates can be made and published anytime.
- **Cost to host:** currently free-tier hosting; no ongoing fees yet.
- **Forms:** Plan a Visit, Prayer Request, and Weekly Encouragement sign-up all
  work and collect responses. The remaining step is turning on automatic email
  notifications so responses reach the right person.
- **Biggest remaining upgrade:** replacing stock photos with real photos of the
  Good Hope family, building, and Pastor Tim.
- **Not yet launched publicly** — we are finishing the foundation first.
